How they filmed Fast and Furious

The idea for the first part of The Fast and the Furious, released in 2001, came to the minds of its creators quite unexpectedly. The thing is that they accidentally came across an article "Racer X" in "Vibe" magazine, where the author wrote about illegal races organized in the largest New York area. At first the movie was planned to be called "Red Line", which means the limiter on the vehicle's speedometer. However, in the end they stopped at the name we know, because it was more appropriate to the spirit of street racing. Consider how the filming of the legendary movie "Fast and Furious" about illegal racers took place.

The main character of Spider-Man

A character in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (KVM) media franchise based on the Marvel Comics character of the same name, commonly known as Spider-Man. Tom Holland first appears as Peter Parker in KVM in The First Avenger: Confrontation, where he is recruited by Tony Stark to help him arrest Captain America and his fugitive Avengers. Producer Kevin Feigy said Parker would be torn between superhero ideologies, saying, "Does he want to be like these other characters? Does he want nothing to do with these other characters? How does that affect his experience, being this down-to-earth but superpowered hero? These are all things that Stan Lee and Steve Ditko played with in the first 10 years of his comics, and that we can now play with for the first time in the movie."
